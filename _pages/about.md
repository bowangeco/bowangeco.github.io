I am broadly interested in how ecosystems maintain, lose, or reorganize their stability under climate change, human management, and spatially extended ecological processes. My previous work examined how long-distance interactions reshape coastal wetland structure and resilience. My current research focuses mainly on dryland ecosystems, especially how water availability, human activities, and internal feedbacks influence ecosystem quality, alternative stable states, and regime-shift risks.

Methodologically, I combine remote sensing, field observations, dynamical systems theory, resilience diagnostics, spatial analysis, and causal inference. I am particularly interested in developing approaches that connect ecological theory with large-scale spatial data, helping to understand how local interactions scale up to landscape-level patterns, functional connectivity, and ecosystem persistence.

## Research Interests

- Ecosystem resilience and regime shifts
- Multistability and alternative stable states
- Spatial interactions and landscape-scale ecological processes
- Dryland ecosystem dynamics and feedbacks
- Remote sensing, spatial analysis, and causal inference
